My favorite things to do when I am abroad are visit new places, try new foods (or old ones with a different twist), and learn more about the unique backgrounds that define Welsh culture. Here is what I have done so far!

Restaurant Recommendations
Restaurant Recommendation Rating
Turtle Bay Great Caribbean food! Chicken and hot sauce were delicious. I wish I had tried the coconut rice. I wasn’t a fan of the salad, however. 4/5
Juboraj Bit of a hike to get there, but once arrived the food was amazing! Though I wish the chicken pakora was bigger, the vindaloo and pilaf rice were decadent! Would recommend this to other Colgate students. 5/5
Masala Bag Gross. This is like the English-Indian Burger King. The Chicken Tikka Masala was sweet (it isn't a sweet curry!), the garlic naan was generic, and there may have been a rock in my Bhatura curry. The worst Indian food I've ever had. 1/5
Slim Chickens Basically like if KFC was better. I had some inferno sauce wings with fries and chicken tenders, and it was pretty good. Wanted some fried food, got some decent fried food. 3/5
Zizzi's This was a fantastically cheap Italian place with a £15 for a starter and main. The pizza I had was delicious and so was the garlic bread. 4/5
Greggs Can't go wrong with a British staple: a Greggs Sausage Roll. 4/5

During my time here, I have learned some interesting facts about Cardiff, Wales, and the UK. Here are four of my favorite ones!

  1. Cardiff is home to many famous celebrities, including Roald Dahl, Ken Follett, Henry Morgan, and even the group Underworld.
  2. Welsh, also known as British, is one of the oldest speaking languages in Europe.
  3. There are multiple reasons why the UK receives a lot of rain compared to other places. The UK is located at a high latitude and is in the path of the North Atlantic Current, a powerful water current that transports warm water up to the isles from the Gulf of Mexico. Paired with a combination of winds coming from all cardinal directions makes the weather cool, wet, and temperate.
    • For more information here's the BBC talking about the UK weather.
  4. Slate and coal mining were very large contributors to the Welsh economy, and as a result there are large piles of slate all across the country.

Places to Visit

There are a number of places I wish to visit around the UK. Within Cardiff, I have yet to visit the iconic Cardiff Castle or head down to the bay. The Castle would be a fun daytrip to do when I am bored, but I also want to swim in the ocean when the weather gets nicer (not during the next storm!). I also want to explore further east and compare that to Cathays or Roath. In Wales, I want to practice my Welsh with someone from North Wales (as we are learning Southern Welsh) and visit Snowdonia, one of the wettest places. In the UK, I want to visit a couple of iconic cities, such as Bath, Liverpool, Manchester, and especially spend a weekend in London. Here's to hoping I can see at least some of these places at some point!

My first impressions

My first impressions of Cardiff and the UK are that it is like being in a dream; where everything is very vaguely familiar but somewhat different and you can't put your finger on it. I love living here and noticing the small differences between American and Welsh culture. One of the things that surprised me the most was how enthusiastic Brits are toward meeting Americans! I was very shocked because I thought Americans were looked down upon in other parts of the world. I guess I was happily proven wrong. I am very excited for what the future holds for me in Cardiff, and I cannot wait to share my experiences with my friends and family back at home.
